Generative AI in Video Games

Introduction

Video games have evolved significantly in recent years, influencing the design of innovative technologies like Generative AI. This technology, which offers challenges, controlled environments, and instant feedback, has become more precise and efficient. Generative AI has become an essential part of the video game industry, enabling developers to create more realistic, immersive, and complex games. While it does not handle the entire development process, its presence is crucial, acting as a co-designer and contributing to the industry's growth and success.

Video games are electronic games where players control images on a video screen, combining chance, competition, and role-playing. The video game industry has evolved significantly since the first console, "The Brown Box," invented in 1967. The development of virtual reality (VR) and artificial intelligence (AI) has significantly influenced the industry. AI is the ability of a digital computer or computer-controlled robot to perform tasks associated with intelligent beings, reducing monotony and eliminating repetitive tasks. In the video game industry, AI plays a dominant role, using artificial neural networks and machine learning techniques. Generative AI, which refers to deep-learning models that generate high-quality content based on trained data, is gaining interest and traction in the industry. As the need for immersive virtuality increases, game makers aim to make games more unpredictable and interactive, using Generative AI to accelerate game production, create better content, and protect intellectual property.

Description of the Technology

Traditional game-making processes are facing challenges in user satisfaction and engaging content, leading to the development of Generative AI in video games. This technology aims to improve character and world interaction, aid problem-solving, and data analysis, promoting rapid and successful game development. Research has shown that AI increases productivity and stability in game creation, allowing developers to take proactive action.

As Generative AI in video games becomes more popular, sustainable, and successful, it will rapidly grow. Venture firm Andreessen Horowitz launched Games Fund in 2022, with many gaming startups planning to use AI in gaming. 87% of studios are using generative AI tools like Midjourney to create in-game environments, with examples like "No Man's Sky" utilizing this technology to create over 18 quintillion unique planets.

Generative AI in video games is primarily implemented through advancements such as Neural Networks, Deep Learning, Modl.ai, Ludo AI, and NVIDIA's GameGAN. Technologies like PANGeA, or Procedural Artificial Narrative using Generative AI, are used for turn-based, role-playing video games, generating content beyond the intended narrative scope. PANGeA generates level data and dialogue based on a prior configuration made by the game developer, and uses a novel validation system to handle free-form text input during game development and gameplay.

Advantages & Disadvantages

AI technology offers several advantages in gaming, including a richer gaming experience, real-time image and sound enhancement, and adaptive difficulty. Generative AI can create new levels, worlds, and environmental situations on the spot, allowing games to be practically infinite and constantly changing. The Finite State Machine (FSM) is a mathematical model used to design systems that change input on output, such as in games like Mario Bros or Call of Duty. AI in audio is an emerging field, but is already used in games like No Man's Sky and Fortnite. AI also adapts to the difficulty of the game based on the player's skill, learning and changing their behavior in real time based on data collected. This allows the game to be more engaging and engaging for users, as it helps them stay engaged and engaged in the game.

The use of Generative AI in video games presents several disadvantages. Users cannot predict the gameplay, as AI's actions depend on each player's intelligence and abilities. This can make games uninteresting and monotonous, as AI-generated NPCs lose their quality. Additionally, the use of AI in content creation raises ethical concerns, such as its impact on the job market for artists, writers, and designers in the video game industry. Automation could lead to lower demand for certain creative roles, making it unethical for AI to perform human work.

Future of the Technology

Generative AI is expected to revolutionize video games with its ability to create immersive and engaging game settings, improve in-game conversations, and create multiple storylines based on player actions and choices. AI will also stand out in non-playable characters (NPCs) and story behavior, allowing players to make decisions based on their actions and choices. This is a significant improvement from traditional video game development cycles, which are costly and time-consuming.

Parallel's "Colony" game uses AI-powered avatars, allowing players to give instructions and advice to AI-powered NPCs. This approach allows for a wide range of gameplay possibilities, even rejecting player suggestions.

Generative AI will also be a significant tool in designing and creating video games, as it can reduce the time and budget needed for traditional development. In the next 20 years, AI is expected to create complex games like "The Legend of Zelda" or "Dark Souls." Additionally, AI will make a difference in online games by modifying the game environment in real time based on player preferences and play styles.

Ethical View

Ethics in the video game industry are being questioned due to the lack of regulation regarding Generative AI. The Gen Alpha generation, aged between 0-13, is the highest user demographic, with 79% playing weekly. The collection of user data by companies and websites is a major concern, as it can be processed, packaged, and sold to third parties for profit. This data can be used for malicious purposes, such as intrusive ads and unsolicited emails. The rise in Generative AI has led to increased demand for data scrapers, which are designed to collect data for training purposes. This raises concerns about the potential for unintentional data scraping without user consent. Text-to-image models like Midjourney are popular examples of Generative AI used in video games.

Text-to-image models use unconsenting and uncompensated artists' videos as training data, causing ethical concerns. Companies like Open AI use millions of videos without permission to profit more than creators would have, while Instagram's parent company Meta allows it to use user's public photos, messages, and captions for AI training. Despite this, many gaming companies have already incorporated Generative AI programs into their games, leading to job losses. Activision laid off 1900 employees, including their 2D art department, and used Midjourney and Stable Diffusion to generate cosmetics for Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 3. Around 90% of companies have already incorporated Generative AI programs into their games, but the ethical issue lies in how companies handle the transition, focusing on cutting labor costs and blinding employees in art, quality testing, and development. Despite the potential benefits of Generative AI, many ethical issues remain to be addressed.

Case Studies and Applications

Procedural Content Generation (PCG) is a set of algorithms or instructions created by game developers to create specific content for the game. It involves the use of randomness, which is not true random numbers but pseudo-random numbers created using an algorithm. The seed is introduced to ensure maximum randomness, and the game itself creates original content for players to explore or use. PCG works by using Perlin noise, an image with black parts and white parts, with the white parts gradually clearing or darker. This image is generated using a complicated math formula, and the clearer the white part, the higher the terrain. A second Perlin noise can be used for the y direction to create cliffs and more interesting terrains. Examples of PCG in games include No Man's Sky, Borderlands, and AI. These games combine PCG with simpler weapon spawn/inventory, making them unique and distinct.

AI in video games has revolutionized character behavior, allowing them to adapt to their environment and situations. For example, "Halo" uses AI to analyze player behavior and adjust tactics, making gameplay more dynamic. Mass Effect also uses AI in NPCs to create more complete characters and make difficult decisions influenced by their personality and motivations.

AI has significantly impacted video games, enhancing gameplay and reducing development resources through advancements in procedural content generation and character behavior. Future possibilities include AI-driven storytelling, which uses algorithms to craft unique experiences for each player based on personal traits, such as playstyle or preferences, and AI matchmaking, which uses AI to match players of equal skill levels and analyze gameplay and statistics to improve multiplayer gaming experiences.
